Pronounciation Guide:


Getting Around:

Especially important on the U-bahn or when generally asking for directions, here are some phrases that might help you get around the city.



Left - links
Right- rechts
North - Nord
East - Ost
South - Süd
West - West
Corner - ecke
Around - um
For example “um die Ecke” = “around the corner”


Directions - Wegbeschreibung
Ticket - karte
Map - Land (or Bahn/Bus/ what you're looking for) Karte
Toilet/bathroom - Toilette


Food, Drinks, and Restaurants:

One of the most important things that may help you is knowing how to count. This will help you understand how much money a person is asking for, how much you get back, and even how much to ask for.



1 - eins
2 - zwei
3 - drei
4 - vier
5 - funf
6 - sechs
7 - sieben
8 - acht
9 - neun
10 - zehn
11 - elf
12 - zwolf
After this people will say the second number then the number of the decade separated by an “and” (“und”).
For example, people will say “funfundzwanzig” which will be 25. The names of the decades are usually the number plus the word “zig” such as dreizig and funfzig. The two exceptions are 10 which is “zehn” (“vierzehn, achtzehn”) and 20 which is “zwanzig” (“einundzwangzig” “zweiundzwangzig”).


When it comes to ordering food, a lot of places might actually have an english menu if you ask. Otherwise, I highly recommend this app called: dict.cc. This is an english/germna dictionary that you can download to work off of wifi or data.
When it comes to actually ordering, you may need to flag down the waiter or they may come to you, you say:
I would like ___, please - Ich mochte ____, bitte.
If you would like a refill or such you can say:
One more, please - Ein mehr, bitte.
When you are ready to pay the bill then you flag down the waiter and say:
I’m ready to pay - Ich bin bereit zu Bezahlen
Can I pay - Kann ich bezahlen?
The bill, please - Die Rechnung, bitte.
Remember that while tipping is not usual in germany, it is sometimes expected that you round up to the nearest whole euro.


When you are checking out in a store you may be asked:
“Tute?” which means that the person is asking if you would like a bag or not. People may also ask if you would like to keep the receipt or not.


Health:

When it comes to your health, the CIEE staff can really be great people to rely on. However, there are some phrases that may be useful to know just in case you’re out and about and need some help.



First off, allergies. It may be simple to look up the translation of what it is you’re allergic to, but it is also important to know how to express those allergies at restaurants and such. As a side note, it may also be smart to carry a card just with information about your allergies and any medication you may be one. This will be helpful for remembering how to say everything and in case you happen to be in a state where you cannot speak or are unconscious.
Allergy phrases:
I am allergic to ____ - Ich bin allergisch gegen ____.
Pollen - Pollen or Blütenstaub
Shellfish - Meeresfrüchte
Gluten - Gluten or Kleber
Nuts - Nüsse
Peanuts - Erdnuss
____ allergy - (ex: peanut allergy) Erdnussallergie


If there are serious problems then the CIEE staff should be able to help set you up with an english speaking doctor, but here are some useful phrases and words for everyday life:
Sick - krank
Cough - husten
